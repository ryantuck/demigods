The "Don't Divide the Sum" Game

ELI5 for Erdős Problem 13

The Game Rules

Imagine you have a bag of numbered balls, from 1 to some number N (like 100). You want to pick as many balls as you can, but there's a tricky rule.

If you pick any three balls, let's call them A, B, and C (where A is the smallest), the sum of the bigger two (B + C) cannot be divisible by the smallest one (A).

For example, if you pick 2, 4, and 6:
Smallest is 2. The others are 4 and 6.
Sum is 4 + 6 = 10.
Does 2 divide 10? Yes! (10 / 2 = 5).
So you cannot have 2, 4, and 6 in your set together.

The Question

Paul Erdős, a famous mathematician who loved asking these kinds of questions, asked: "What is the maximum number of balls you can pick without breaking this rule?"

It turns out the answer is roughly one-third of the total balls. So if you have 100 balls, you can pick about 33 of them safely.

The Formal Proof

We have formalized the statement of this problem in the Lean programming language. This allows computers to verify the math exactly.
